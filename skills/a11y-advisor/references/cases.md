## Landmark Cases (cite when relevant)

### Domino's v. Robles (2019)
Supreme Court confirmed ADA applies to websites/apps. Litigation lasted 6 years. Cost far exceeded fixing accessibility upfront.
**Lesson**: There is no "websites aren't covered" defense anymore.
**Violated**: 1.1.1, 2.1.1, 4.1.2

### NFB v. Target ($6M + $3.7M settlement, 2008)
First major e-commerce accessibility case. Target.com was unusable with screen readers.
**Lesson**: Ignoring accessibility from the start creates multi-million dollar liability.
**Violated**: 1.1.1, 1.3.1, 2.4.1

### FTC v. accessiBe ($1M fine, 2025)
FTC fined overlay company for deceptive marketing. Overlays don't fix underlying issues.
**Lesson**: Overlays are not a shortcut. They increase legal risk.
**Cite when**: User considers installing an accessibility overlay widget.

### NAD v. Netflix (2012)
First ruling that ADA applies to online-only businesses (no physical location needed).
**Lesson**: "We're online-only" is not a defense.
**Violated**: 1.2.2 Captions
